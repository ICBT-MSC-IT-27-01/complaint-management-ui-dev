CompliMate CMS Design System: Login Page
1. Vision & Strategy
The goal of this design system is to establish a secure, professional, and highly legible interface for the CompliMate Complaint Management System. It prioritizes clarity and trust, ensuring that both workforce members and clients can easily navigate the authentication process.

2. Visual Principles
Clarity over Clutter: Every element on the screen serves a specific purpose. Whitespace is used strategically to reduce cognitive load.
Security & Trust: The design uses structured layouts and professional motifs to reinforce the system's reliability.
Accessibility: High contrast ratios and clear typography ensure the system is usable for everyone.
3. Typography
Primary Typeface: Inter
Usage:
Headings: Bold weights (700) for clear section identification.
Body Text: Regular weights (400) for general information and form labels.
Interactive Elements: Medium weights (500) for buttons and tabs to indicate clickability.
4. Color Strategy (Agnostic of Original UI)
Primary: A deep, trustworthy blue is used for primary actions (Sign In) and brand-critical elements.
Secondary/Surface: Light grays and off-whites are used for backgrounds to create depth and separate the login card from the page.
Feedback:
Success: Emerald Green for positive confirmation.
Error: Crimson Red for clear warning states.
Contrast: Text colors are chosen to maintain a minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against their backgrounds.
5. Components & Layout
Desktop Layout
Split-Screen Concept: A two-pane layout where the left side contains the functional login card and the right side provides brand context and key features.
Login Card: A centered, elevated white card containing:
Multi-tab navigation (Workforce vs. Client).
Clear input fields with icons for context.
Action links for "Forgot password" and "Contact support".
Mobile Layout
Single Column: A stacked approach focused on the login form.
Full-Width Actions: Buttons are full-width to accommodate thumb-friendly interaction.
Brand Presence: Logo and system name are prominently placed at the top to anchor the experience.
6. Interaction States
Hover: Subtle darken or lighten effects on buttons and links.
Focus: Clear blue rings around input fields to indicate active typing status.
Disabled: Muted gray states for buttons when required fields are missing.
